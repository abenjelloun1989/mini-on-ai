# AI Automation Agency Launch Playbook

This playbook is for technical beginners who've explored n8n and want to turn that skill into a real agency business. Follow these steps to land your first clients, productize your services, and scale past $10K/month.

---

## 1. Define a Hyper-Specific Niche

Generalist automation agencies lose to specialists every time. Pick one industry and one pain point to own completely.

- **Target a vertical with money and pain:** Real estate agencies wasting 10 hours/week on lead follow-up, e-commerce brands manually handling refund requests, or SaaS companies struggling with onboarding sequences are all high-value targets.
- **Validate before building:** Post in 3 industry-specific Facebook Groups or Slack communities. Ask: "How are you currently handling [X process]?" If 10+ people respond with complaints, you've found your niche.
- **Example:** "We automate lead nurturing for independent mortgage brokers" is 10x more powerful than "We do business automation."

---

## 2. Build Three Core Service Packages

Don't sell custom projects — sell outcomes at fixed prices. This makes sales predictable and delivery repeatable.

- **Starter ($500–$1,500):** One workflow, one integration, one use case. Example: Auto-send a personalized follow-up email when a new lead fills out a form (Typeform → OpenAI → Gmail via n8n).
- **Growth ($2,500–$5,000):** Multi-step automation with CRM integration, AI enrichment, and a 30-day support window. Example: Inbound lead → qualify with AI → route to Slack → log in HubSpot → trigger drip sequence.
- **Retainer ($1,500–$3,000/month):** Ongoing management, new workflows each month, priority support. This is your bread-and-butter revenue.

Build one live demo for each package using real tools before you pitch a single client.

---

## 3. Land Your First 3 Clients in 30 Days

Your first clients won't come from cold ads. They come from warm outreach and visible expertise.

- **Mine your existing network first:** Message 20 small business owners you already know. Offer to audit their current workflows for free on a 30-minute Loom video. End every audit with a specific recommendation tied to one of your packages.
- **Cold outreach with proof:** Use Apollo.io or LinkedIn Sales Navigator to find 50 targets in your niche. Send a 3-sentence DM: identify their pain, reference a specific example of your work, propose a free 20-minute call. Expect a 5–10% reply rate.
- **Post one case study per week:** Even if your first client is yourself, document the before/after. "I reduced my invoice follow-up time from 4 hours to 12 minutes using this n8n workflow" is a client magnet on LinkedIn.

---

## 4. Deliver Efficiently With Repeatable Systems

Profitability dies in scope creep and rework. Systematize delivery from day one.

- **Use a project template:** Kick-off call → requirements doc (use a Notion template) → build → internal test → client UAT → handoff documentation. Never skip documentation — it's your proof of work and your upsell lever.
- **Build a workflow library in n8n:** After every project, save your core sub-workflows as reusable components. A lead enrichment block, an AI classification node, a Slack notification template — each one cuts your next build time by 30–40%.
- **Set clear scope boundaries in writing:** "This package includes one trigger, up to three action nodes, and one AI step. Additional integrations are billed at $150/hour." Send this before work begins, not after a dispute starts.

---

## 5. Price for Value, Not for Hours

Charging hourly caps your income and commoditizes your work. Price based on the outcome you're delivering.

- **Calculate ROI before quoting:** If a client spends 15 hours/week on manual data entry at an effective rate of $50/hour, that's $3,000/month in lost productivity. Your $2,500 automation pays for itself in the first month.
- **Anchor high, then tier down:** Present your Growth package first. Most clients will either take it or ask for the Starter — they rarely go straight to the cheapest option if you lead with value.
- **Add a $300–$500 setup fee to retainers:** It filters out low-commitment clients and offsets your onboarding time. Clients who pay setup fees churn far less than those who don't.

---

## 6. Scale Past $10K/Month

Once you have 3–5 paying clients, the growth levers are referrals, productization, and delegation.

- **Systematize referrals:** After every successful delivery, send a one-sentence ask: "Do you know one other [mortgage broker/e-commerce operator] who struggles with [X]? I'll give you a $300 referral credit." Two referrals per client per quarter compounds fast.
- **Create a micro-SaaS or template product:** Package your best workflow as a $97–$297 n8n template on Gumroad or a niche marketplace. It generates passive income and inbound leads simultaneously.
- **Hire a junior VA or automation assistant at $15–$25/hour** for documentation, testing, and client communication. You stay in strategy and sales; they handle execution. This doubles your client capacity without doubling your hours.

---

## Quick-Reference Summary

- **Niche specificity** beats generalism — pick one industry, one pain point
- **Three fixed packages** (Starter/Growth/Retainer) make selling and delivery predictable
- **First 3 clients** come from warm network + targeted cold outreach + public case studies
- **Workflow library** in n8n cuts build time by 30–40% per project
- **Price on ROI**, lead with your mid-tier package, always charge a setup fee on retainers
- **Scale levers:** structured referrals, productized templates, delegated execution
