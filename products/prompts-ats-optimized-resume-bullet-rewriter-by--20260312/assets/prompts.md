# ATS-Optimized Resume Bullet Rewriter by Job Description

> Prompts that help job seekers paste any job posting and transform weak, generic resume bullets into keyword-rich, metric-driven lines that pass applicant tracking systems.

---

## Prompts (25 total)

### 1. Core Bullet Rewriter from Job Description

**Use case:** Use this as your go-to starting prompt when you have a job description and want to overhaul an entire section of your resume bullets at once.

```
I need you to rewrite my resume bullets to be ATS-optimized for a specific job posting. Here is the job description:

[PASTE FULL JOB DESCRIPTION HERE]

Here are my current resume bullets:
[PASTE YOUR CURRENT BULLETS HERE]

Please rewrite each bullet by: (1) incorporating exact keywords and phrases from the job description, (2) adding measurable metrics where I can insert them (use placeholders like [X%] or [$ amount] where I need to fill in numbers), (3) starting each bullet with a strong action verb that matches the job description's language, (4) keeping each bullet under 2 lines, and (5) prioritizing the top skills and requirements mentioned in the job posting. Return the rewritten bullets in a numbered list with a brief note on which keywords were added.
```

### 2. Keyword Gap Analyzer Before Rewriting

**Use case:** Use this before rewriting to get a strategic map of exactly which keywords to target, so your rewriting efforts are focused and data-driven.

```
Before I rewrite my resume, I need to identify the keyword gaps between my current resume and this job description.

Job Description:
[PASTE FULL JOB DESCRIPTION HERE]

My Current Resume Text:
[PASTE YOUR FULL RESUME OR RELEVANT SECTION HERE]

Please: (1) Extract the top 20 hard skills, soft skills, tools, certifications, and industry-specific terms from the job description, (2) Identify which of those keywords are missing or underrepresented in my resume, (3) Flag which missing keywords are likely ATS eliminators (appear multiple times or in the job title/requirements sections), and (4) Suggest exactly where in my resume each missing keyword can be naturally inserted. Format your response as a table with columns: Keyword | Found in Resume | Priority Level | Suggested Placement.
```

### 3. Single Weak Bullet Transformer

**Use case:** Use this when you have one particularly weak or generic bullet that needs special attention, or when you want multiple options to choose from for a critical role.

```
Transform this single weak resume bullet into 3 ATS-optimized versions tailored to the job description below.

Job Description:
[PASTE FULL JOB DESCRIPTION HERE]

Weak Bullet:
[PASTE YOUR SINGLE WEAK BULLET HERE]

For each of the 3 versions, please: (1) Use a different strong action verb, (2) Incorporate specific keywords from the job description, (3) Include a metric placeholder if none exists (e.g., 'resulting in [X]% improvement'), (4) Vary the structure so I can choose the best fit. After the 3 versions, explain in one sentence what makes each version stronger than the original.
```

### 4. Metric Injection Specialist

**Use case:** Use this when your bullets describe responsibilities but lack quantifiable achievements — the most common reason resumes fail both ATS and human review.

```
My resume bullets lack measurable results. Please help me add metrics to make them ATS-friendly and compelling.

Job Description:
[PASTE FULL JOB DESCRIPTION HERE]

My Bullets Without Metrics:
[PASTE YOUR BULLETS HERE]

For each bullet: (1) Rewrite it with a metric placeholder in the most logical position, using formats like [increased by X%], [saved $X], [managed team of X], [reduced time by X hours/week], [generated $X in revenue], etc. (2) Ask me one specific question per bullet to help me find the real number (e.g., 'How many clients did you manage?' or 'What was the before/after comparison?'). (3) Also incorporate relevant keywords from the job description into each rewritten bullet. Format as: Original → Rewritten Bullet → Question to Find Your Metric.
```

Add a note explaining the translation strategy for each bullet.

### 10. ATS Score Estimator and Rewriter

**Use case:** Use this when you want a diagnostic view of your resume's ATS performance alongside the rewrite, helping you understand WHY changes are made.

```
Please analyze how well my current resume bullets would perform against this job description's likely ATS filters, then rewrite them to improve the score.

Job Description:
[PASTE FULL JOB DESCRIPTION HERE]

My Current Resume Bullets:
[PASTE YOUR CURRENT BULLETS HERE]

Step 1 — Score My Current Bullets: Rate my current bullets from 0-100 on ATS compatibility based on: keyword match rate, use of exact phrases from JD, clarity of skills, and absence of ATS-unfriendly formatting.

Step 2 — Identify Top 10 ATS Keywords: List the 10 most critical keywords/phrases from the job description that ATS systems would likely screen for.

Step 3 — Rewrite for Maximum ATS Score: Rewrite each bullet to naturally incorporate the missing keywords while maintaining authenticity and readability.

Step 4 — Score the Rewritten Bullets: Rate the rewritten version and explain the improvement. Show me: Before Score | After Score | Key Changes Made.
```

### 20. ATS-Safe Formatting and Language Audit

**Use case:** Use this as a final quality control pass before submitting your resume — catches subtle ATS-killer errors that most people miss.

```
Please audit my resume bullets for ATS compatibility issues beyond just keywords, and rewrite any problematic bullets.

Job Description:
[PASTE FULL JOB DESCRIPTION HERE]

My Current Resume Bullets:
[PASTE YOUR CURRENT BULLETS HERE]

Please check for and fix ALL of the following ATS issues: (1) Acronyms — expand all abbreviations and add the spelled-out version in parentheses the first time (e.g., 'Search Engine Optimization (SEO)'), (2) Symbols — flag any %, $, &, or symbols that may not parse correctly and suggest text alternatives, (3) Tense consistency — standardize to past tense for previous roles, present tense for current role, (4) Passive voice — rewrite any passive constructions into active, (5) Vague language — replace phrases like 'various,' 'multiple,' 'several' with specific numbers, (6) Keyword density — ensure top 5 keywords from the JD appear at least once each, (7) Verb quality — replace weak verbs like 'helped,' 'assisted,' 'worked on' with ownership verbs. Return: Issue Found | Original Bullet | Corrected Bullet.
```
