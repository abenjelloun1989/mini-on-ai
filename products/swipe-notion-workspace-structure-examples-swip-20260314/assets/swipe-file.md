# Notion Workspace Structure Examples Swipe File for Team Leads

> Team leads use this swipe file of 25 real Notion workspace architectures — organized by team size and function — to make confident structural decisions instead of starting from a blank sidebar.

---

### 1. 5-Person Startup Team (Pre-Product-Market Fit)

🏠 Home
├── 📋 Weekly Standup Notes
├── 🎯 Company OKRs (Q3 2024)
└── 📣 Announcements

🚀 Product
├── 🗺️ Product Roadmap
├── 🐛 Bug Tracker
├── 💡 Feature Requests
└── 🧪 Experiment Log

👥 Customers
├── 🗂️ CRM (Contacts Database)
├── 📞 Call Notes
└── 💬 Feedback Vault

💰 Growth
├── 📊 Metrics Dashboard
├── 🧲 Acquisition Channels
└── ✉️ Email Campaigns

⚙️ Operations
├── 📁 SOPs
├── 🔑 Credentials Vault (linked to 1Password)
└── 💸 Expense Log

📚 Team Wiki
├── 🙋 Who We Are
├── 📐 How We Work
└── 🛠️ Tools & Stack

*Use this for seed-stage startups with 3-6 people wearing multiple hats. The flat, minimal structure prevents over-engineering. Home acts as the daily driver. Avoid sub-nesting more than 2 levels deep until headcount exceeds 8.*

├── 📢 Announcements (read-only, manager posts)
├── 🕐 Working Hours & Timezone Map

📅 Rhythm

├── 🔁 Retrospectives Archive
├── 📊 Quarterly Review Docs
└── 🧭 Monthly All-Hands Notes

🔨 Projects

│   ├── Project Alpha
│   ├── Project Beta
│   └── Project Gamma
├── ⏸️ On Hold
└── ✅ Completed Archive

├── 🙋 Team Directory (with timezone, focus hours, Slack handle)
├── 🤝 How We Communicate
├── 🛠️ Tools We Use

└── 🆕 New Hire Start Here

💬 Water Cooler

*The Decision Log is the backbone of async-first teams — document every significant decision with context so late joiners understand the 'why.' The Open Questions Board prevents decisions from stalling. Water Cooler section compensates for the absence of hallway chats. Pair this with a strict 'write it down' culture norm.*

├── ✅ Onboarding Kickoff Template
└── 📞 Check-In Call Agenda

📚 Enablement & Resources

Each customer account page should have: CSM owner, contract value, renewal date, last contact date, health score. The Churn Post-Mortem Log — when filled in consistently — becomes your most valuable data set for product and sales.*
