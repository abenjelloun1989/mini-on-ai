### 4. Email Sequence Copywriting Proposal (Welcome Series)

PROPOSAL: Welcome Email Sequence for Northbrook Coffee Roasters
Prepared by: James Okafor, Email Copywriter
Date: [Date]

---

OPPORTUNITY
Every new subscriber who joins your list is at peak interest — they just discovered Northbrook, they're curious, and they haven't bought yet. A strong welcome sequence turns that curiosity into a first purchase. Right now, new subscribers get one generic confirmation email. We can do a lot better.

THE SEQUENCE: 6 EMAILS OVER 12 DAYS

Email 1 (Immediate): The warm welcome. Brand story, what to expect, gentle introduction to your most popular roast. CTA: Browse the shop.

Email 2 (Day 2): The origin story. Where your beans come from and why it matters. Builds emotional connection. CTA: Learn about our sourcing.

Email 3 (Day 4): Social proof. Customer reviews, press mentions, the '10,000 happy mornings' angle. CTA: See what people are saying.

Email 4 (Day 6): Education. 'How to brew Northbrook coffee the right way' — positions your brand as the expert and increases perceived value. CTA: Download brewing guide (if you have one) or explore blends.

Email 5 (Day 9): Objection handling. Addresses 'why pay more for specialty coffee?' Speaks directly to the hesitant subscriber. CTA: Try a starter pack.

Email 6 (Day 12): Soft offer. A welcome discount or free shipping on first order — reserved for those who haven't purchased yet. CTA: Claim your offer.

DELIVERABLES
• 6 fully written emails (subject line, preview text, body, CTA)
• 2 subject line variations per email for A/B testing
• Klaviyo-ready formatting notes
• Recommended send time and segment logic

INVESTMENT: $1,800
50% upfront, 50% on delivery
Turnaround: 10 business days from completed questionnaire

NEXT STEP
Reply 'yes' and I'll send the agreement today.

*Perfect for e-commerce brands with an email list but no nurture sequence. The email-by-email breakdown does the selling for you — clients can visualize exactly what they're getting. Subject line variations and Klaviyo notes show you understand their tools and world.*
