# Client Onboarding Checklist for Independent Consultants

> Solo consultants starting a new engagement get a repeatable 30-step checklist covering intake forms, tool access, stakeholder intros, and deliverable alignment to eliminate chaotic project kickoffs.

---

## Preparation

- [ ] **Send and collect a signed contract or statement of work before any work begins**
  *Establishes legal protection, defines scope boundaries, and prevents scope creep from day one. Never start work on a handshake alone.*

- [ ] **Issue and collect a completed client intake form covering business goals, pain points, budget, and timeline**
  *Forces the client to articulate their needs in writing, surfaces misalignments early, and gives you a reference document throughout the engagement.*

- [ ] **Confirm payment terms, invoicing schedule, and preferred payment method in writing**
  *Eliminates payment disputes later. Clarify net-30 vs. net-15, accepted methods (ACH, credit card, check), and late-payment penalties upfront.*

- [ ] **Request and review any existing documentation, reports, or prior work relevant to the engagement**
  *Prevents duplicating work already done, reveals institutional knowledge gaps, and helps you speak credibly in early stakeholder meetings.*

- [ ] **Create a dedicated project folder structure in your file system or cloud storage before receiving any client assets**
  *A consistent folder structure (e.g., /Contracts, /Assets, /Deliverables, /Communications) keeps you organized and makes handoffs clean at project end.*

- [ ] **Set up a dedicated communication channel and confirm the client's preferred method (email, Slack, Teams, etc.)**
  *Reduces missed messages and context-switching. Agree on response time expectations so neither party feels ignored or overwhelmed.*

- [ ] **Request access credentials or invitations to all tools, platforms, and systems needed for the engagement**
  *Tool access delays are one of the most common causes of lost billable time in week one. Identify and request access at least 3–5 days before kickoff.*

- [ ] **Confirm your primary point of contact and identify who has final decision-making authority on deliverables**
  *Prevents the frustration of delivering work only to learn the true decision-maker has different expectations. Know who can say yes.*

## Execution

- [ ] **Schedule and conduct a formal kickoff meeting with all key stakeholders before beginning any deliverables**
  *Aligns everyone on goals, roles, and timelines simultaneously. Reduces the risk of conflicting instructions from different team members.*

- [ ] **Document and distribute a kickoff meeting summary including agreed goals, next steps, owners, and deadlines within 24 hours**
  *Creates a shared written record that prevents 'but I thought we agreed' disputes. Makes accountability visible to everyone from the start.*

- [ ] **Introduce yourself via email to all secondary stakeholders or team members you will work with during the engagement**
  *Builds rapport early, signals professionalism, and ensures supporting team members know who you are before you need something from them.*

- [ ] **Build and share a project timeline or milestone map showing key deliverable dates and review checkpoints**
  *Gives the client visibility into progress and creates natural accountability moments. Use a simple Gantt chart or shared task board if needed.*

- [ ] **Define and document the deliverable review and approval process including number of revision rounds allowed**
  *Unlimited revisions are a profit killer. Specify in writing how many rounds of feedback are included and what constitutes a change-order trigger.*

- [ ] **Establish a weekly or bi-weekly standing check-in cadence with your primary point of contact**
  *Regular touchpoints prevent small misalignments from becoming major problems. They also reassure the client that progress is being made.*

- [ ] **Verify that all tool access, data permissions, and system integrations are functioning correctly within the first 48 hours**
  *Access issues discovered on day one are easier to resolve than those discovered mid-project when you are blocked on a deliverable with a deadline.*

- [ ] **Log all key decisions, change requests, and scope additions in a running decision log shared with the client**
  *Provides a defensible audit trail if scope disputes arise. Clients often forget verbal approvals; a written log protects both parties.*

## Review

- [ ] **Conduct a mid-engagement check-in to assess satisfaction, re-confirm priorities, and flag any emerging risks**
  *Catching dissatisfaction at the midpoint gives you time to course-correct. Surprises at final delivery damage client relationships and referrals.*

- [ ] **Deliver a final summary document that maps completed deliverables back to the original goals stated in the intake form**
  *Demonstrates tangible value and reminds the client of the full scope of work completed. Strengthens the case for testimonials and renewals.*

- [ ] **Offboard all access by revoking or transferring credentials, tools, and permissions upon project completion**
  *Protects the client's security posture and protects you from liability for any post-engagement system issues. Document all transfers in writing.*

- [ ] **Send a close-out survey or request a testimonial within one week of project completion while the experience is fresh**
  *Timely feedback captures honest impressions and generates social proof. Delayed requests result in vague or forgotten responses that are less useful.*
