    Frequently Asked Questions

    What is an ATS?

    An Applicant Tracking System (ATS) is software used by employers to filter job applications. It scans resumes for keywords, skills, and formatting to rank candidates before a human recruiter reviews them. Over 90% of large companies use an ATS.

    What is a good ATS score?

    A score above 75 is generally considered good and means your resume is likely to pass automated screening. Below 50 means significant keyword gaps that could cause your application to be filtered out. The goal is to match as many relevant keywords from the job description as possible without keyword stuffing.

    How does this checker work?

    Our AI analyzes your resume text against the job description to identify keyword matches, missing skills, section structure issues, and formatting problems. The free check gives you your score, matched keywords, and basic tips. The paid analysis ($5) includes all missing keywords, rewritten bullet points optimized for ATS, and detailed section-by-section feedback.
